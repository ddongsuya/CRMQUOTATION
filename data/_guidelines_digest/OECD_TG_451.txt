[1] 2. The majority of carcinogenicity studies are carried out in rodent species, and this Test Guideline is intended therefore to apply primarily to studies carried out in these species. Should such studies be required in non-rodent species, the principles and procedures outlined in this Guideline together with those outlined in OECD TG 409, Repeated Dose 90-day Oral Toxicity Study in Non-Rodents (6) should be applied, with appropriate modifications. Further guidance is available in the OECD Guidance Document No. 116 on the Design and Conduct of Chronic Toxicity and Carcinogenicity Studies (7).

[7] 21. Guidance on all aspects of dose selection and dose level spacing is provided in Guidance Document No. 116 (7). At least three dose levels and a concurrent control should be used. Dose levels will generally be based on the results of shorter-term repeated dose or range finding studies and should take into account any existing toxicological and toxicokinetic data available for the test chemical or related materials.
